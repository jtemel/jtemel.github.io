About

Me

I am a third year at the University of Toronto, pursuing a specialist in computer science and a major in mathematics as an undergraduate student. I am fascinated with software development, machine learning and abstract algebra. Over the next year, I will be mining and analyzing years worth of online discussion board content and PCRS data to benchmark exercises for introductory computer science courses. More information on the project can be found under the Research tab. I previously worked as a Teaching Assistant for CSC236 (Introduction to the Theory of Computation) and MAT223 (Linear Algebra I), and as a tutor for CSC207 (Software Design), over the fall term. Currently, I am working as a teaching assistant for CSC209 (Software Tools and Systems Programming) [2 sections].

Personal

Over the last twelve years, I have actively been playing and self-teaching various musical instruments. I began with drums at the age of 8, picked up the guitar at the age 12, and most recently, learnt to play the trumpet at the age of 16. I have also been producing electronic music with Ableton Live since I was 14. I try to read a new book from the stacks at UofT's Robarts Library every month -- you can find the list of books read here.

Academia

After the completion of my second year at UofT, I was awarded the University of Toronto Honour Roll for achieving 90% or greater in two or more computer science courses. In addition, I was awarded the "Golden Deer" Learning Excellence Award for an outstanding job on my CSC263 (Data Structures and Analysis) final exam.

Over the next year I will be an active member in the mathematics club and the computer science reading group. Below is a full list of all the courses I have taken thus far at the University of Toronto.
